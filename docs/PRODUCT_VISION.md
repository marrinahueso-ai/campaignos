# CampaignOS — Product Vision

## One-Line Vision

CampaignOS is the AI communications operating system for PTO volunteers — turning school calendars into organized, on-brand campaigns that parents actually see.

## The Problem

PTO volunteers are not marketers. They are parents who stepped up to help their school community. Every year they face the same burden:

- A crowded school calendar with dozens of dates that need promotion
- Fragmented communication across Facebook, Instagram, newsletters, websites, and flyers
- Limited time, inconsistent messaging, and no single place to plan it all
- Last-minute scrambling before events because reminders were never scheduled
- Board approval bottlenecks with no clear checklist or audit trail

Most "social media tools" assume a dedicated marketing team. PTOs need something different: a calm, calendar-first assistant that helps volunteers communicate clearly — without requiring design or copywriting skills.

## What CampaignOS Is

CampaignOS is an **AI communications assistant built for PTO volunteers**.

It is **not** a generic social post generator. It is a **calendar-first operating system** that helps PTOs:

1. **Import or enter** their school calendar
2. **Turn dates into events** with structured context (audience, theme, location, goals)
3. **Open an Event Workspace** for each event — a single home for everything related to that event's communications
4. **Generate full campaigns** with AI: captions, newsletter blurbs, website copy, artwork prompts, reminder timelines, and approval checklists
5. **Review, approve, and publish** with confidence — knowing nothing was missed

CampaignOS respects how PTOs actually work: volunteer-led, board-governed, calendar-driven, and community-focused.

## What CampaignOS Is Not (V1)

- Not a general-purpose marketing platform for businesses
- Not a replacement for school district official communications
- Not an auto-publishing bot that posts without human review
- Not a design tool (it generates prompts and copy; volunteers still choose what to publish)
- Not multi-organization enterprise software (V1 is PTO-only)

## Target User (V1)

**Primary:** PTO Communications Chair or volunteer responsible for promoting events

**Secondary:** PTO board members who review and approve outgoing messages

**Organization:** Parent-Teacher Organizations at individual schools (one PTO workspace per school)

## Core Product Principles

### 1. Calendar-First

The school calendar is the source of truth. CampaignOS starts with dates — not blank pages. Volunteers should feel like they are *organizing* communications, not *inventing* them from scratch.

### 2. Event Workspace

Every event gets a dedicated workspace. All copy, reminders, approvals, and assets for that event live in one place. No hunting across tabs, docs, and group chats.

### 3. AI as Assistant, Not Autopilot

AI drafts; humans decide. Every generated asset is editable. Approval workflows keep the board in control. CampaignOS reduces workload — it does not remove accountability.

### 4. PTO-Appropriate Tone

Generated content should sound like a helpful parent volunteer, not a corporate brand. Warm, clear, inclusive, and action-oriented — appropriate for school communities.

### 5. Clarity Over Complexity

Volunteers have 30 minutes on a Tuesday night. The product should surface today's priorities, upcoming deadlines, and what needs approval — not overwhelm with dashboards full of vanity metrics.

## The Event Workspace (Concept)

Each event workspace is the heart of CampaignOS. It contains:

| Area | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Event Details** | Title, date, time, location, audience, theme, status |
| **Campaign Assets** | AI-generated captions, newsletter blurbs, website copy |
| **Artwork Prompts** | Descriptions volunteers can use in Canva or with a designer |
| **Reminder Timeline** | Suggested schedule: save-the-date, weekly reminder, day-before, day-of |
| **Approval Checklist** | Board sign-off items before anything goes live |
| **Activity Log** | Who created, edited, approved, or published what |

## Success Looks Like

A PTO Communications Chair opens CampaignOS at the start of the school year, uploads the calendar, and within an hour has draft campaigns for the first six events — each with copy, reminders, and an approval checklist ready for the board.

Board members spend minutes reviewing — not hours rewriting.

Parents receive consistent, timely information about PTO events across the channels the PTO actually uses.

## Long-Term Direction (Beyond V1)

- Multi-channel scheduling and publishing integrations
- Template libraries by event type (carnival, fundraiser, teacher appreciation)
- Volunteer role management and permissions
- Analytics on reach and engagement (only where useful to PTOs)
- Expansion to other school volunteer groups (booster clubs, room parents) — after PTO is nailed

## V1 Focus Statement

**Version 1 exists to prove one thing:** a PTO volunteer can go from school calendar → structured events → AI-generated communication campaigns → board approval — in a single, calm product experience.

Everything else waits until that loop works beautifully.
