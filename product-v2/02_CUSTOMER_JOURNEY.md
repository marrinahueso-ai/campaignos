# CampaignOS 2.0 — Customer Journey

**Primary Persona:** Jamie — PTO Communications Chair  
**Secondary Persona:** Dana — PTO President / Board Reviewer  
**Last updated:** June 2026

---

## Who Jamie Is

Jamie is a working parent who volunteered to "help with social media" at the first PTO meeting of the year. She is capable, organized, and cares deeply about her school community. She is not a marketer. She has never written a content calendar. She receives the school calendar as a PDF in August and spends the next ten months trying not to let anyone down.

Jamie's emotional baseline is **anxious competence** — she will get it done, but she wishes someone would tell her exactly what "done" looks like.

---

## Journey Overview

```
Discover → Onboard → Import Calendar → Review Events →
Open First Workspace → See Auto Timeline → Review Generated Assets →
Board Approval → Publish → Weekly Rhythm → End of Year
```

Every stage must answer: **"What should I do next?"**

---

## Stage 1: Discovery & First Impression

**Jamie's question:** *"Is this actually for people like me?"*

**What she sees:**
- A homepage that speaks to PTO volunteers, not marketing agencies
- Language about calendars, school years, and decision fatigue — not "content creation"
- Social proof from other PTO Communications Chairs (future)
- A clear promise: upload your calendar, get a communication plan

**What she feels:**
> "This wasn't built for influencers. It was built for me."

**Success criteria:**
- Jamie understands within 30 seconds that this is a *system*, not a chatbot
- She believes the product will think ahead for her

**Failure mode to avoid:**
- Landing page that looks like another AI writing tool
- Feature lists instead of outcomes

---

## Stage 2: Organization Setup

**Jamie's question:** *"Does CampaignOS know our school?"*

**What she sees:**
- A guided setup flow (already started in v1 School Setup)
- School name, PTO name, district, mascot, principal
- Brand assets: logos, colors, fonts
- Communication tone preferences
- Default Communication Playbook settings for the organization

**What she does:**
1. Enters school and PTO identity
2. Uploads logos and brand colors
3. Confirms default tone ("friendly and welcoming")
4. Reviews pre-loaded playbook templates (Book Fair, PTO Meeting, Teacher Appreciation)

**What she feels:**
> "It already feels like our school's communications hub — not a generic template."

**Success criteria:**
- Setup completes in under 15 minutes
- Jamie sees that playbooks will drive her timelines automatically
- She does not feel like she is configuring software — she is setting up *her school's voice*

**Reduces workload by:**
- Capturing brand and tone once, reused everywhere
- Eliminating "what should our posts sound like?" for every event

---

## Stage 3: School Year & Calendar Import

**Jamie's question:** *"Can I get the whole year in without retyping everything?"*

**What she sees:**
- A School Year selector (e.g., 2025–2026)
- Calendar import screen with upload, paste, or manual options
- A review dashboard showing extracted events with categories and confidence
- Clear next action: confirm events to create workspaces

**What she does:**
1. Selects the active school year
2. Uploads the district PDF calendar
3. Reviews extracted events on the Calendar Import Review screen
4. Confirms, edits, or removes events
5. Clicks "Import All" or reviews individually

**What she feels:**
> "I uploaded one PDF and now I have 24 events with communication plans waiting for me. I would have spent weeks on this."

**Success criteria:**
- Most events captured with minimal correction
- Each confirmed event automatically receives an event type and inherited playbook
- Jamie sees progress: "24 events ready · 3 need review"

**Reduces workload by:**
- Eliminating manual event-by-event data entry
- Auto-assigning event types and playbooks
- Creating workspaces in bulk, not one at a time

---

## Stage 4: The Dashboard — Daily Command Center

**Jamie's question:** *"What should I do next?"*

**What she sees:**
- A calm, spacious dashboard — not a wall of widgets
- **Today's Actions** — the 1–3 things due today, ranked by urgency
- **This Week** — upcoming communication timeline items across all events
- **Waiting on Board** — approvals pending from Dana
- **Upcoming Events** — next events on the calendar with workspace status
- **Year Progress** — a gentle view of how much of the year is planned vs. complete

**What she does:**
1. Opens CampaignOS on a Tuesday evening
2. Sees: "Post Book Fair 7-day reminder on Facebook · Approve Teacher Appreciation flyer"
3. Clicks the first action — lands directly in the right workspace, right asset, right screen
4. Completes both tasks in 18 minutes

**What she feels:**
> "I didn't have to figure out where to start. It told me."

**Success criteria:**
- Dashboard never shows fake data or vanity metrics
- Every item is clickable and leads to the exact next action
- Empty states teach: "Import your calendar to see your year at a glance"

**Reduces workload by:**
- Replacing "what was I supposed to do?" with a prioritized action list
- Eliminating the need to check five channels manually

---

## Stage 5: Opening an Event Workspace

**Jamie's question:** *"Everything for this event — where is it?"*

**What she sees:**
- A hero banner: event name, date, countdown, status, category, owner
- Five clear sections below:
  1. Event Overview
  2. Communication Timeline
  3. Communications Hub (generated assets by channel)
  4. Creative Assets
  5. Activity & History
- A progress ring: "4 of 9 communications ready · 2 approved · 1 published"

**What she does:**
1. Clicks "Fall Book Fair" from the Events list or Dashboard
2. Lands in the workspace — not an edit form, a *project home*
3. Scans the timeline and sees exactly what's due when
4. Opens the next due item

**What she feels:**
> "This is my command center for the book fair. I'm not jumping between Facebook, Canva, and email."

**Success criteria:**
- One event = one URL = one source of truth
- Jamie can assess event readiness in 10 seconds
- The workspace feels premium — spacious cards, clear hierarchy, no clutter

**Reduces workload by:**
- Consolidating 5+ tools into one workspace
- Showing progress so Jamie knows what's left

---

## Stage 6: Communication Timeline (The Magic Moment)

**Jamie's question:** *"When am I supposed to say what?"*

**What she sees:**
- A vertical timeline auto-generated from the event's Communication Playbook
- Example for Book Fair: 30 days · 14 days · 7 days · 3 days · Day Before · Day Of · Thank You
- Each node shows: due date, channel, status, and preview of content
- Color-coded status: upcoming · due today · overdue · complete · waiting on approval

**What she does:**
1. Opens the Book Fair workspace
2. Sees the full timeline already populated from the Book Fair playbook
3. Clicks "7 days out — Facebook post" — sees generated draft ready to review
4. Edits one sentence, approves, marks for publishing

**What she feels:**
> "I didn't have to plan the schedule. CampaignOS already knew when to remind parents."

**Success criteria:**
- Timeline generates automatically when event is created
- Rescheduling the event date shifts all timeline nodes
- Jamie never manually creates a "reminder schedule"

**Reduces workload by:**
- Eliminating the #1 hidden task: planning *when* to communicate
- Replacing spreadsheet calendars with an intelligent, event-aware timeline

---

## Stage 7: Generated Assets & the AI Brain

**Jamie's question:** *"What should the post actually say?"*

**What she sees:**
- Communications Hub with cards for each channel: Website, Newsletter, Facebook, Instagram, Email, Flyer, Principal Notes, Morning Announcements, Volunteer Signup
- Each card shows: status, last updated, preview snippet
- Actions: Generate · Preview · Edit · Approve
- Creative Assets section: Hero Image, Square Graphic, Instagram Story, Flyer, Logo, Documents

**What she does:**
1. Clicks "Generate All" or generates individual channels
2. Reviews AI-drafted copy that sounds like a PTO volunteer, not a corporation
3. Edits lightly — changes ticket price, adds volunteer link
4. Previews how it will look on each channel

**What she feels:**
> "This is 90% there. I tweaked it in two minutes instead of writing it from scratch."

**Success criteria:**
- Generated content reflects event context (audience, theme, location, school voice)
- Jamie edits more than she writes
- Regenerating one channel does not destroy edits on others

**Reduces workload by:**
- Replacing blank-page writing with light editing
- Generating channel-appropriate variants automatically

---

## Stage 8: Board Approval (Dana's Journey)

**Dana's question:** *"Can I trust what will go out in our school's name?"*

**What she sees:**
- A notification: "3 communications waiting for your review"
- Approval queue showing event, channel, preview, and checklist
- One-click approve, or request changes with a note
- Audit trail: who submitted, when, what changed

**What she does:**
1. Opens the Approval Center from an email link or Dashboard
2. Reviews the Book Fair Facebook post and flyer
3. Checks the board checklist (principal notified, no conflicting events)
4. Approves both in under 5 minutes

**What she feels:**
> "I can see exactly what families will see. No surprises on our Facebook page."

**Success criteria:**
- Dana never needs to email Jamie for context
- Approval takes minutes, not meetings
- Jamie is notified instantly of any requested changes

**Reduces workload by:**
- Replacing email chains and text threads with a structured approval flow
- Giving the board confidence without adding friction

---

## Stage 9: Publishing

**Jamie's question:** *"It's approved — now what?"*

**What she sees:**
- Publishing Center showing approved items ready to go live
- Copy-to-clipboard for each channel
- Step-by-step guidance: "Paste into Facebook · Mark as Published"
- Timeline updates automatically when items are marked published

**What she does:**
1. Opens Publishing Center or the specific timeline item
2. Copies the approved Facebook caption
3. Posts manually to the PTO Facebook page
4. Returns to CampaignOS and clicks "Mark as Published"
5. Timeline shows the item as complete; Dashboard removes it from Today's Actions

**What she feels:**
> "Done. Next item is in 5 days. I don't have to remember."

**Success criteria:**
- Publishing feels like closing a loop, not starting a new task
- Published items are visually locked and logged
- The next due item surfaces automatically

**Reduces workload by:**
- Closing the loop so nothing falls through the cracks
- Auto-advancing the volunteer to the next action

---

## Stage 10: Weekly Rhythm & End of Year

**Jamie's weekly question:** *"Am I on track?"*

**What she sees:**
- Dashboard with a calm weekly view
- No guilt-inducing red alerts — clear, actionable priorities
- Year Progress: "18 of 24 events complete · 6 upcoming"
- Archived events from past months, searchable for next year

**What she feels at year-end:**
> "We communicated better this year than ever before. And I didn't burn out."

**Success criteria:**
- CampaignOS is useful from August through June
- Rescheduling events updates timelines automatically
- Past events inform next year's playbooks (future)

## Key Moments That Matter

| Moment | Emotional shift | Why it matters |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| First calendar import | Overwhelm → Relief | Jamie decides if CampaignOS saves her year |
| First auto-generated timeline | Confusion → Confidence | Jamie sees the system thinks ahead |
| First AI generation | Skepticism → Trust | Jamie decides if copy is "good enough" |
| First board approval | Anxiety → Control | Dana decides if the product is trustworthy |
| First published post | Effort → Closure | Jamie closes the loop and builds a habit |
| First Monday Dashboard check | Chaos → Calm | Jamie builds a weekly rhythm |

---

## Empty States as Guidance

Every empty screen teaches the next step:

| Empty state | Message | Action |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| No organization | "Set up your school's communication hub" | Start School Setup |
| No school year | "Choose the year you're planning" | Select school year |
| No calendar | "Import your school calendar to begin" | Upload calendar |
| No events | "Your calendar events will appear here" | Import or create |
| No timeline | "Assign a playbook to generate your timeline" | Choose event type |
| Nothing to approve | "Approved communications will appear here" | — |
| Nothing to publish | "You're all caught up" | — |

---

## Out of Scope for 2.0 Launch

- Multi-school district administration
- Direct auto-posting without human review
- Volunteer shift scheduling
- Ticketing and payments
- Non-PTO organization types
