# CampaignOS 2.0 — Analytics

**Purpose:** Define what analytics means in CampaignOS — proof of value, not vanity metrics.  
**Last updated:** June 2026

---

## The Analytics Philosophy

PTO Communications Chairs do not need a growth dashboard.

They are not optimizing click-through rates. They are not A/B testing subject lines. They are not reporting to a CMO.

They need to answer three questions:

1. **Did we communicate on time?**
2. **Did we cover every event?**
3. **Can I show the board we did our job?**

CampaignOS analytics exist to answer those three questions — and nothing more.

---

## What Analytics Is Not

- Not Facebook engagement metrics (likes, shares, reach)
- Not follower growth tracking
- Not competitive benchmarking
- Not real-time social listening
- Not a reason to open CampaignOS daily

Engagement data may come in future integrations. It is not the core of 2.0 analytics.

---

## What Analytics Is

**Operational proof** that the PTO communication plan was executed.

Think of it as a **year-end report card** for the Communications Chair — generated automatically, not assembled in a panic in June.

---

## Primary Audience

| Persona | What they need from analytics |
|---------|------------------------------|
| **Jamie (Communications Chair)** | Confidence she didn't miss anything |
| **Dana (PTO President)** | Proof the PTO communicated effectively |
| **Next year's Chair** | Understanding of what was done last year |
| **Board (annual report)** | Summary for PTO meeting presentation |

## Year-Level Analytics

### What the user sees

A **School Year Summary** accessible from Dashboard (collapsed section) or Settings:

```
2025–2026 Communication Summary

Events managed:          24
Events fully complete:   22  (92%)
Communications planned:  168
Communications published: 154  (92%)
On-time publish rate:    94%
Average approval time:   1.4 days

Channels used:
  Facebook ············ 48 posts
  Newsletter ·········· 32 entries
  Website ············· 28 updates
  Morning Announcements  24 reads
  Instagram ··········· 18 posts
  Email ··············· 4 sends

Busiest month: October (Book Fair + Fall Festival)
Longest approval: Teacher Appreciation (2.8 days)
```

### Why it exists

Jamie walks into the June PTO meeting with a one-page summary. Dana presents it to the board. Nobody asks "did we communicate enough this year?" — the data answers it.

### Reduces workload by

Eliminating the end-of-year scramble to remember what was posted where.

## Organizational Memory Analytics (Future)

Analytics extends beyond the current school year into **year-over-year institutional memory**.

### What the user sees (future)

**Prior-year comparison** on recurring events:

```
Fall Book Fair — Year over Year

                    2024–2025    2025–2026
Communications:     7            7
On-time rate:       88%          94%
Channels used:      5            6
Approval cycle:     2.1 days     1.2 days
Resources linked:   3            5
Lessons noted:      "Start volunteer drive earlier"
```

**Org Memory dashboard** (future):
- Events cloned from prior years
- Playbook adjustments inherited from past execution
- Creative assets reused or modernized via Creative Intelligence
- Lessons learned captured at event completion

### Why it exists

Analytics should not reset every August. Next year's Chair sees what worked — not just what happened last June.

### Not in current sprint

Organizational Memory analytics are **future build**. See `13_INNOVATION_BACKLOG.md`, `05_EVENT_WORKSPACE.md`.

## Event-Level Analytics

### What the user sees

At the bottom of each Event Workspace — a compact **Event Summary** (visible when event is complete or archived):

```
Fall Book Fair · Communication Summary

Timeline:       7 of 7 nodes complete
Published:      7 of 7 communications
On time:        6 of 7 (1 published 1 day late)
Approval cycle: 1.2 days average
Channels:       Facebook, Instagram, Website, Newsletter, Email, Announcements
Duration:       Aug 15 – Oct 5 (51 days)
```

### Why it exists

Jamie reviews a completed event and sees a clean record. Next year's Chair inherits context: "Last year's Book Fair used 7 communications over 51 days — all channels covered."

**Organizational Memory footer** (future):
- Link to prior-year same event
- Clone timeline and resources action
- Lessons learned field captured at completion

## Dashboard Analytics (Lightweight)

The Dashboard includes subtle operational metrics — not a separate analytics page:

| Metric | Display | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Year progress | "18 of 24 events on track" | Are we covering the year? |
| On-time rate | "94% published on schedule" | Are we hitting deadlines? |
| This month | "6 communications due · 4 complete" | Current pace |
| Board backlog | "0 items awaiting approval" | Is governance flowing? |

These appear as small badges and progress bars — not charts or graphs.

---

## Timeline Analytics

Within the Communication Timeline, each node records:

| Data point | Example |
|------------|---------|
| Planned date | Sep 27 |
| Published date | Sep 27 |
| On time? | Yes |
| Channels published | Facebook, Email |
| Channels skipped | None |
| Approval duration | 8 hours |

Timeline analytics power the on-time rate calculation.

## Approval Analytics

| Metric | Value | Audience |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Average approval time | 1.4 days | Jamie (is Dana responsive?) |
| Items approved first submission | 85% | Jamie (is content quality good?) |
| Items requiring changes | 15% | Jamie (where to improve) |

These are operational — not performance reviews.

## What We Deliberately Exclude (V2)

| Excluded metric | Why |
|-----------------|-----|
| Facebook likes/reach | Requires platform API; not core to PTO value |
| Follower growth | Vanity metric; doesn't measure communication quality |
| Email open rates | Requires email integration; future consideration |
| "Best performing post" | Optimization framing inappropriate for volunteers |
| Competitor benchmarking | PTOs don't compete |
| Time saved estimates | Hard to measure accurately; feels gimmicky |

---

## Future Analytics (Post-2.0)

When platform integrations arrive:

| Future metric | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Facebook post reach | "340 families saw the Book Fair reminder" |
| Instagram story views | Proof of visibility for the board |
| Email open rate | Newsletter effectiveness |
| Engagement by event type | "Book Fair posts get 2x engagement — invest more" |

These supplement operational analytics — they don't replace them.

One-page PDF summarizing:

Jamie exports this for the June PTO meeting. Dana presents it. The board sees tangible value.

## Empty States

**Mid-year, few events complete:**
> "Your year summary will build as you complete event communications. 4 events in progress."

**No events:**
> "Import your calendar to start tracking your communication year."

Encouraging, not empty.

---

## Visual Design

Analytics in CampaignOS should feel:
- **Calm** — soft progress bars, not aggressive charts
- **Readable** — numbers with labels, not data visualization for its own sake
- **Board-ready** — clean enough to screenshot for a meeting
- **Secondary** — analytics support the workflow; they don't dominate it

No dark analytics dashboards. No red/green stock tickers. This is a school PTO, not a trading floor.

---

## Success Criteria

Jamie finishes the school year and can answer:
- Did we cover every event? **Yes — 22 of 24 complete.**
- Did we communicate on time? **94% on-time rate.**
- Can I show the board? **Here's the year summary.**

Three questions. One screen. No spreadsheet required.

---

## Relationship to the Operating System

Analytics is the **final layer** of the hierarchy:

```
Organization → School Year → Calendar → Events → Workspace →
Timeline → Assets → Approval → Publishing → Analytics
```

It closes the loop by proving the system worked — for Jamie, for Dana, and for next year's volunteer who inherits a communication operation, not a blank slate.

**Future layer:** Organizational Memory connects analytics across school years — clone, compare, and learn from prior-year execution. See `13_INNOVATION_BACKLOG.md`.
