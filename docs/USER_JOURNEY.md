# CampaignOS — User Journey

## Primary Persona: Jamie — PTO Communications Chair

Jamie is a working parent who volunteered to handle the PTO's social media and newsletters. She has no marketing background. She gets the school calendar as a PDF in August and spends the year trying to keep parents informed without burning out.

---

## Journey Overview

```
Sign Up → Set Up PTO Profile → Add School Calendar → Review Events →
Open Event Workspace → Generate Campaign → Review & Edit → Board Approval →
Publish / Share → Repeat Across the Year
```

---

## Stage 1: First Visit & Onboarding

**Goal:** Jamie understands what CampaignOS does and feels confident it was built for PTOs, not marketers.

**Entry point:** Referral from another PTO, school Facebook group, or direct search.

**Actions:**
1. Lands on CampaignOS homepage
2. Reads value proposition: calendar-first AI assistant for PTO communications
3. Creates an account (or signs in)
4. Completes PTO profile setup: school name, organization name, tone preferences, default hashtags

**Thoughts:**
> "This looks like it was made for people like me — not a big marketing team."

**Success criteria:**
- Jamie completes setup in under 10 minutes
- She understands the product is calendar-first, not a blank social post tool

**Pain points to avoid:**
- Asking for too much information upfront
- Jargon-heavy onboarding
- Fake demo data that doesn't reflect PTO reality

---

## Stage 2: Calendar Intake

**Goal:** Jamie gets the school year's dates into CampaignOS without manual retyping everything.

**Actions:**
1. Navigates to Calendar intake
2. Chooses one of:
   - **Upload** a school calendar (PDF, image, or spreadsheet)
   - **Paste** a list of dates and event names
   - **Enter manually** key dates one at a time
3. AI extracts or structures dates into draft events
4. Jamie reviews extracted events, edits titles/dates, and confirms

**Thoughts:**
> "I uploaded the PDF and it pulled out Fall Carnival, Book Fair, and Teacher Appreciation Week. I just need to fix two dates."

**Success criteria:**
- Most calendar dates are captured with minimal correction
- Jamie sees a list of draft events ready to become campaigns
- She feels ahead of the year — not behind

**Pain points to avoid:**
- Poor extraction accuracy with no easy correction UI
- Forcing Jamie to create events one-by-one with no bulk path

---

## Stage 3: Dashboard — Daily Command Center

**Goal:** Jamie opens CampaignOS and immediately knows what needs attention today.

**Actions:**
1. Opens Dashboard
2. Sees four sections:
   - **Today's Priorities** — tasks due today (e.g., "Approve Fall Carnival day-before reminder")
   - **Upcoming Events** — next events on the calendar
   - **Campaigns Needing Approval** — drafts waiting for board review
   - **Recent Activity** — log of changes across the workspace
3. Clicks into the most urgent item

**Thoughts:**
> "I have 20 minutes before pickup. I can approve two posts and schedule one reminder."

**Success criteria:**
- Dashboard reflects real data — no fake metrics
- Empty states guide Jamie when nothing exists yet
- Jamie never wonders "what should I do next?"

---

## Stage 4: Event Workspace

**Goal:** Jamie manages all communications for one event in a single place.

**Actions:**
1. Opens an event from Dashboard or Events list
2. Enters or confirms event details: title, description, date, time, location, audience, theme
3. Opens the Event Workspace tabs/sections:
   - **Overview** — event summary and status
   - **Campaign Assets** — generated copy
   - **Reminder Timeline** — suggested communication schedule
   - **Approval Checklist** — board sign-off items
   - **Activity** — change history

**Thoughts:**
> "Everything for Spring Carnival is here. I'm not jumping between Google Docs, Canva, and Facebook."

**Success criteria:**
- One event = one workspace = one source of truth
- Jamie can see what's done vs. what's still needed at a glance

---

## Stage 5: AI Campaign Generation

**Goal:** Jamie generates a full communication package without writing from scratch.

**Actions:**
1. Clicks "Generate Campaign" inside the Event Workspace
2. AI produces:
   - Social captions (Facebook, Instagram variants)
   - Newsletter blurb
   - Website / homepage copy
   - Artwork prompt (for Canva or a volunteer designer)
   - Reminder timeline (save-the-date, 2-week, 1-week, day-before, day-of)
   - Approval checklist (board review items)
3. Jamie edits any section inline
4. Saves draft or submits for approval

**Thoughts:**
> "This isn't perfect, but it's 90% there. I tweaked the tone and added the ticket price."

**Success criteria:**
- Generated content sounds like a PTO volunteer, not a corporation
- All asset types are present in one generation pass
- Jamie edits less than she writes

**Pain points to avoid:**
- Generic copy that ignores event context (audience, theme, location)
- Regenerating one asset and losing edits on others
- No clear distinction between draft and approved content

---

## Stage 6: Board Approval

**Goal:** Board members review and approve communications without email chains.

**Secondary persona:** Dana — PTO President**

**Actions:**
1. Dana receives notification (email or in-app) that campaigns need review
2. Opens **Campaigns Needing Approval** on Dashboard
3. Reviews each asset type in the Event Workspace
4. Checks off approval checklist items
5. Approves, requests changes, or rejects with a note

**Thoughts:**
> "I can see exactly what will go out before it goes out. No surprises on the Facebook page."

**Success criteria:**
- Clear approval status per event and per asset
- Audit trail of who approved what and when
- Jamie gets notified of requested changes

---

## Stage 7: Publish & Share

**Goal:** Jamie gets approved content into the channels the PTO uses.

**Actions (V1 — manual share workflow):**
1. Copies approved captions to clipboard
2. Posts to Facebook / Instagram manually
3. Pastes newsletter blurb into school newsletter submission
4. Marks items as published in CampaignOS
5. Reminder timeline shows what's coming next

**Thoughts:**
> "I copied the approved post, published it, and marked it done. Next reminder is in 5 days."

**Success criteria:**
- Jamie can mark assets as published without leaving the workspace
- Reminder timeline updates based on what's been sent
- No approved content accidentally gets re-edited after publish

**Future (post-V1):** Direct scheduling integrations with Facebook, Instagram, and email tools.

---

## Stage 8: Ongoing Year Management

**Goal:** Jamie runs the full school year without starting over each time.

**Actions:**
1. Adds new events as the calendar changes
2. Archives past events
3. Reuses successful campaign patterns (future)
4. Checks Dashboard weekly for priorities and approvals

**Thoughts:**
> "The whole year is mapped. When something gets rescheduled, I update one event and the reminders adjust."

**Success criteria:**
- CampaignOS stays useful from August through June
- Rescheduling an event updates related reminders
- Past events are archived but searchable

---

## Key Moments That Matter

| Moment | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| First calendar upload | Jamie decides if CampaignOS saves her time |
| First AI generation | Jamie decides if the copy is "good enough" to edit, not rewrite |
| First board approval | Dana decides if the product is trustworthy |
| First published post | Jamie closes the loop and becomes a retained user |
| First Monday morning Dashboard check | Jamie builds a habit |

---

## Empty States as Guidance

Every empty section should teach Jamie what to do next:

- **No calendar yet** → "Upload your school calendar to get started"
- **No events** → "Create your first event or import dates from your calendar"
- **No campaigns** → "Generate a campaign for your next event"
- **Nothing to approve** → "Approved campaigns will appear here for board review"
- **No activity** → "Your team's actions will be logged here"

---

## Out of Scope for V1 Journey

- Multi-school district management
- Direct auto-posting to social platforms
- Payment / ticketing integration
- Volunteer shift scheduling
- Non-PTO organization types
